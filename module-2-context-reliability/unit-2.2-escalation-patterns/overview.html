Task Statement 5.2 — Design escalation triggers and ambiguity resolution strategies for AI agents

⏱ 2 min read → then open the Lab

The Escalation Decision Tree

When should an AI agent hand off to a human? The exam rewards explicit trigger-based escalation, not sentiment analysis.

ConditionEscalate?Why
Customer explicitly requests human YES — immediately Customer preference overrides all other signals. Do NOT investigate first.
Policy gap (request not covered by policy) YES Agent cannot invent policy. Escalate with structured context.
No progress after N attempts YES Define N concretely (e.g., 3 tool calls with no resolution).
Frustrated customer, simple issue NO — resolve first Offer resolution; escalate only if they insist after solution offered.
Agent self-reports low confidence UNRELIABLE Model confidence is not calibrated for escalation decisions.

The pattern: escalation triggers must be explicit and deterministic — based on observable conditions (customer said "talk to a human," policy lookup returned empty, retry count exceeded threshold), not inferred sentiment or self-assessed confidence.

💡 Key Insight: Sentiment-based escalation fails in both directions: frustrated customers with simple issues get over-escalated (wasting human time), while calm customers with genuinely complex issues get under-escalated (poor outcomes). The exam always prefers explicit trigger conditions over sentiment analysis.
⚠ Exam Trap: "Analyze customer sentiment to decide when to escalate" sounds sophisticated but is almost always a distractor. Sentiment correlates poorly with escalation need. The correct answer defines explicit conditions: customer requests human, policy gap detected, or resolution attempts exhausted.
